CBC Parameters — What They Mean
WBC — White Blood Cells
Measures immune cells that fight infections. Normal: 4.5–11 ×10³/µL
Low → Leukopenia, Bone marrow failure High → Infection, Leukemia
RBC — Red Blood Cells
Carries oxygen throughout the body. Normal: 4.5–5.5 ×10⁶/µL
Low → Anemia, Blood loss High → Polycythemia, Dehydration
HGB — Hemoglobin
Oxygen-carrying protein in RBCs. Normal: 12–17 g/dL
Low → Anemia, Iron deficiency High → Polycythemia, Lung disease
HCT — Hematocrit
% of blood volume occupied by RBCs. Normal: 36–50%
Low → Anemia, Overhydration High → Polycythemia, Dehydration
MCV — Mean Corpuscular Volume
Average size of RBCs. Normal: 80–100 fL
Low → Microcytic anemia, Iron deficiency, Thalassemia High → Macrocytic anemia, B12/Folate deficiency
MCH — Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin
Average hemoglobin per RBC. Normal: 27–33 pg
Low → Hypochromic anemia, Iron deficiency High → Macrocytic anemia, B12 deficiency
MCHC — Mean Corpuscular Hgb Concentration
Hemoglobin concentration in RBCs. Normal: 32–36 g/dL
Low → Hypochromic anemia, Thalassemia High → Hereditary spherocytosis
PLT — Platelets
Tiny cells that help blood clot. Normal: 150–400 ×10³/µL
Low → Thrombocytopenia, Dengue, ITP High → Thrombocytosis, Myeloproliferative disorder
Differential Parameters
NEU — Neutrophils (50–70%)
First responders to bacterial infection.
Low → Neutropenia, Viral infection High → Bacterial infection, Sepsis
LYM — Lymphocytes (20–40%)
Key cells for immune memory and viral defense.
Low → Immunodeficiency, HIV High → Viral infection, CLL, Lymphoma
MON — Monocytes (2–8%)
Clean up dead cells and fight chronic infections.
Low → Bone marrow suppression High → Chronic infection, Monocytic leukemia
EOS — Eosinophils (1–4%)
Responds to allergies and parasitic infections.
Low → Usually not clinically significant High → Asthma, Allergy, Parasitic infection
BAS — Basophils (0–1%)
Involved in allergic and inflammatory responses.
Low → Hyperthyroidism, Stress High → Allergic reaction, CML
Additional Indices
RDW — Red Cell Distribution Width (11.5–14.5%)
Variation in RBC size — helps differentiate anemia types.
High → Mixed anemia, Iron + B12 deficiency
MPV — Mean Platelet Volume (7.5–12.5 fL)
Average size of platelets — indicates platelet production rate.
Low → Bone marrow failure High → ITP, Platelet destruction, Thrombosis risk
PDW — Platelet Distribution Width (9–17%)
Variation in platelet size — high PDW indicates platelet activation.
High → Thrombocytopenia, ITP, Dengue
